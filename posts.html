Tussie-mussie

Meaning

(n) A small bouquet of flowers tied together with a ribbon or ornament used as a gift on occassions.

Etymology

The word has been derived from the bouquets used during the reign of Queen Victoria. It was originally called nosegay.

Dissuade

Meaning

(v) To discourage someone from doing something

Etymology

The word has been derived from Latin word dis meaning reverse of and uadere meaning to persuade someone to do something.

Perfidious

Meaning

(adj) One who cannot be trusted.

Etymology

The word has been derived from Latin word perfidiosus meaning untrustworthy.

Dissimulate

Meaning

(v) To hide one's character, feelings, motives or objectives.

Etymology

The word has been derived from Latin word dissimulare meaning hide.

Fremdscham

Meaning

(n) The feeling of embarrassment asa result of the activites of someone else.

Etymology

The word has been derived from the German

  • Adjective fremd meaning strange.
  • Noun scham meaning embarrassment or shame.

Beguile

Meaning

  • (v) To enchant someone to deceive him or her.
  • To spend time in a good way

Etymology

The word has been derived from the English words be and guile meaning to deceive someone.

Bungle

Meaning

(v) To fail to do something properly because of mistakes or carelessness.

Etymology

The word has been derived from the Scandanavian word equivalent to a Swedish term bengel meaning strike.

Seraphic

Meaning

  • (adj) Blissful
  • Beautiful and pure

Etymology

The word has been derived from the Hebrew word equivalent seruphim meaning angels of the highest order.
